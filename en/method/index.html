Method

The method間

Three phases, one non-negotiable rule and one common-sense test. Nothing more. Especially not.

The three phases

  1. 01
    Mapping

    Field diagnostic, interviews with business teams and IT, risk mapping. Four to six weeks. Deliverables: audit report, risk map, prioritised roadmap, ROI calculations per use case.

  2. 02
    Workshop

    Scoping of the selected use case, rapid prototyping, go/no-go validation. Written acceptance criteria, shared success conditions, steering committee in place.

  3. 03
    Implementation

    Industrialisation, production rollout, governance. Planned skills transfer, documented run, metrics in place.

The non-negotiable rule

No project without a Decision-maker.

An identified sponsor, a responsive decision committee, transparency on blockers. Without that, we launch neither workshop nor production.

Identify the critical decisions

Technology choices, ROI validation, stage go/no-go, incident handling, scope changes.

Assign the RACI roles

Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, Informed. Not a chart, a commitment.

Document and share

Signed RACI matrix, regular committees, formalised change control.

The napkin test

Before any project, a quick calculation.

A single sheet is enough. It is a filter, not a proof. The proof is the detailed analysis that follows, with assumptions documented.

Formula

(Gain × Frequency × Users × Hourly rate) > Build cost + Annual run cost

Example

15 min/day for 20 people at €40/h = €73,000/year in theoretical gain. If build + run = €30,000, the ROI is positive.

Not counted

Change management, training, integration into the IS, run stabilisation. To be added for a complete calculation.
